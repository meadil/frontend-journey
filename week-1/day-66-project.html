Classic Homemade Margherita Pizza

A simple Italian pizza with a crispy crust, tangy tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella, and basil. Perfect for Friday nights when you want something better than delivery.

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

For the Sauce:

  • 1 can crushed tomatoes (14 oz)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Toppings:

  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Olive oil for drizzling
  • Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Make the dough - Mix warm water, yeast, and sugar in a bowl. Let it sit for 5 minutes until foamy.
  2. Add flour, salt, and olive oil to the yeast mixture. Mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Place dough in an oiled bowl, cover with a towel, and let it rise for 1-2 hours until doubled in size.
  5. While dough rises, make the sauce - Heat olive oil in a pan, add garlic, cook for 1 minute. Add crushed tomatoes and season with oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 15 minutes.
  6. Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). If you have a pizza stone, put it in the oven now.
  7. Punch down the dough and divide it into 2 portions for two pizzas.
  8. Roll out each portion into a 12-inch circle on a floured surface.
  9. Transfer dough to a baking sheet or pizza peel. Spread sauce evenly, leaving a 1-inch border for the crust.
  10. Add mozzarella slices evenly across the pizza.
  11. Bake for 12-15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.
  12. Remove from oven and immediately top with fresh basil leaves and a drizzle of olive oil.

Equipment Needed:

  • Large mixing bowl
  • Rolling pin
  • Baking sheet or pizza stone
  • Medium saucepan
  • Measuring cups and spoons

Pro Tips:

  • Let the dough come to room temperature before rolling for easier stretching
  • Don't overload with toppings or the crust will be soggy
  • For extra crispy crust, brush the edges with olive oil before baking
  • Use a pizza stone if you have one - it makes a huge difference

Variations:

Pepperoni Pizza
Add pepperoni slices on top of the cheese before baking
White Pizza
Skip the tomato sauce, use ricotta and mozzarella instead, add garlic
Veggie Supreme
Add bell peppers, mushrooms, onions, and olives on top of the sauce
BBQ Chicken
Use BBQ sauce instead of tomato sauce, add cooked chicken and red onions
